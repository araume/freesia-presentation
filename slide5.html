Literature Survey

Our research is grounded in existing studies that demonstrate the potential and challenges of using AI for emotional support and mental health applications.

Year Author/s Title Methodology Findings Future Works
2021 Thompson, R. & Bell, A. "Chatbots for Mental Health: A New Frontier" analytics Case Study Analysis Users reported feeling heard and supported by conversational agents. Develop more empathetic NLP models for nuanced support.
2022 Wang, L. "Online Mental Health Communities: A Safe Space?" poll Survey & Content Analysis Participants valued anonymity and peer support in online forums. Strengthen moderation policies and peer support training.
2022 Evans, M. "Using LLMs in Counseling Simulations" science Experimental Design LLMs provided responses rated similarly to human counselors in empathy. Explore real-world deployment under supervision.
2023 Sharma, D. & Li, J. "Emotional Intelligence in AI: How Much Is Enough?" compare Comparative Evaluation Higher EI scores in LLMs correlated with better user satisfaction. Develop benchmarks for emotional performance in LLMs.
2024 Garcia, F. "Hybrid Support Systems: Humans and AI Working Together" groups Mixed Methods Combining human therapists with AI tools enhanced accessibility. Scale hybrid systems in underserved communities.
